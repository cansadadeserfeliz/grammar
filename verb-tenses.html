  • We are going to Tunja tomorrow. [Present Continuous]
  • What are you doing? [Present Continuous]
estar + gerundio Present Continuous
  • Juan está viendo la televisión.
  • Está lloviendo.
  • Estoy leyendo un libro de García Márquez.
  • Este invierno está lloviendo mucho.
  • Esta noche voy al cine. [Presente]
  • John is watching TV.
  • It is raining.
  • I'm reading a book by García Márquez.
  • It is raining a lot this winter.
  • I am going to the cimema this night.

  • Tu hermana acaba de llegar. [Presente]
  • Todavía no he visto la nueva película de Almodóvar.

  • Hace cinco meses no lo vemos. [Presente]
  • We were at home today. [Past Simple]
  • We had lunch at half past two. [Past Simple]
  • I've been in the office this week.

  • Your sister has just came.
  • I haven't watched the new film by Almodóvar yet.

  • I haven't seen Luisa lately, have you?
  • I've never been in Perú.
  • They've always wanted to go to Venezuela.
  • Have you ever been in his/her house?
  • We've lived in London for ten years.
  • I haven't been here for a long time.
  • She's been here since Monday.
  • We haven't seen him for five months.
llevar + gerundio Present Perfect Continuous
  • Llevo gafas desde niño. [Presente]
  • Llevo leyendo tres horas. [Presente: estar + gerundio]
  • ¿Llevas mucho tiempo esperando? [estar + gerundio]
  • I've been wearing glasses since I was a kid.
  • I've been reading for three hours.
  • Have you been waiting long?
Pretérito indefinido Past Simple
  • Ayer fuimos al cine.
  • El otro día vi a Pilar en la calle.
  • La semana pasada dejé de fumar.
  • El 2007 fui a Venezuela.
  • Have un año vi un ovni.
  • We went to the cinema yesterday.
  • I saw Pilar in the street the other day.
  • I gave up smoking last week.
  • I went to Venezuela in 2007.
  • I saw an UFO a year ago.
estar [Pretérito indefinido] + gerundio Past Continuous
  • Estaba duchándome cuando sonó el teléfono.
  • Estuvieron estudiando hasta las 2 de la mañana.
  • I was having a shower when the telephone rang.
  • They were studying until 2 in the morning.
Pretérito Imperfecto Past Continuous / Past Simple / used to + infinitive
  • Llovía mucho aquella noche.
  • Pensábamos que no vendrías.
  • Ella escribía a su madre todos los días.
  • Mi abuelo siempre llevaba una boina.
  • Siempre iban a Dublín en Navidad.
  • It was raining hard that night. [Past Continuous]
  • We thought you wouldn't come. [Past Simple]
  • She used to write to her mother every day. [used to]
  • My grandfather used to wear beret all the time. [used to]
  • They used to go to Dublin for Cristmas. [used to]
Pretérito Pluscuamperfecto Past Perfect
  • Cuando llegué Pedro había comido ya.
  • When I arrived Pedro had already had lunch.
llevar [Pretérito Imperfecto] + gerundio Past Perfect Continuous
  • Había estado esperando su carta mucho tiempo.
    → Llevaba mucho tiempo esperando su carta.
  • He had been waiting for her letter for a long time.
Futuro Futuro simple Future Simple
  • Mañana hará sol.
  • No saldrás hasta que termines tus deberes.
  • ¿Qué hora será?
  • —¿Dónde estará mi monedero?
    —Estará en el bolso.
  • La boda será en julio.
  • It will be sunny tomorrow.
  • You will not leave until you finish your homework.
  • I wonder what the time is? [Present Simple]
  • —Where's my purse?
    —It must be in your handbag. [Present Simple]
  • The wedding will be in july.
Futuro perfecto Future Perfect
  • Cuando llegues ya habré terminado todo el trabajo.
  • When you arrive I will have already finished all the work.

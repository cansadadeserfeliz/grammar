Español vs Inglés

Articles in Spanish vs Articles in English

Indefinite Articles, Definite Articles, No Article (∅).

Inglés Español
Singular countable nouns
  1. Bye! Have a nice day.
  2. Do you need an umbrella?
  3. She never wears a hat.
  4. What a beautiful day!
  5. I've got a stomach ache.
  6. That's a nice table.
  7. When I was a child I used to be very shy.
  8. I don’t have a car.
  1. Adiós, ¡que tengas un buen día!
  2. ¿Necesitas una sombrilla?
  3. Ella nunca lleva ∅ sombrero.
  4. ¡Qué día ∅ tan bonito!
  5. Tengo ∅ dolor de estómago. (= Me duele el estómago.)
  6. Esa es una buena mesa.
  7. Cuando yo era ∅ niña era muy tímida.
  8. No tengo ∅ carro.
Singular vs plural
  1. A dog is an animal.
  2. I'm an optimist.
  3. Tom's father is a doctor.
  4. Are you a good driver?
  5. Jane is a really nice person.
  6. What a lovely dress!
  7. Jack has a long nose.
  1. Un perro en un animal.
  2. Soy una [persona] optimista.
    (Se puede decir: soy ∅ optimista.)
  3. El padre de Tom es ∅ doctor.
  4. Eres una buena conductora?
    (Se puede decir: Eres buena ∅ conductora?)
  5. Ella es una persona amable.
    (Se puede decir: Ella es ∅ amable.)
  6. Que vestido ∅ tan bonito!
  7. Juan tiene la nariz larga.
  1. Dogs are ∅ animals.
  2. We're ∅ optimists.
  3. Most of my friends are ∅ students.
  4. Jane's parents are ∅ really nice people.
  5. What ∅ awful shoes!
  6. Jack has ∅ blue eyes.
  1. Los perros son ∅ animales. (Todos los perros.)
  2. Somos ∅ optimistas.
    (Se puede decir: Somos unas personas optimistas. Somos unos optimistas.)
  3. La mayoria de mis amigos son ∅ estudiantes.
    (Pero: La mayoria de mis amigos son unos estudiantes de medicina/juiciosos.)
  4. Los padres de Juana son ∅ personas amables.
    (Se puede decir: Los padres de Juana son unas personas amables.)
  5. Qué ∅ zapatos tan horribles!
  6. Juan tiene ∅ ojos azules.
    (Se puede decir: Juan tiene los ojos azules.
    Pero: Juan tiene ∅ lunares/pecas (siempre sin articulo).)
Some = a number of / a few of / a pair of
  1. I've seen some good movies recently.
  2. Some friends of mine are coming to stay at the weekend.
  3. I need some new sunglasses. (=a new pair of sunglasses)
  1. Recientemente vi unas buenas películas.
  2. Unos amigos míos se quedaron para el fin de semana.
  3. Necesito unas gafas de sol nuevas.
A/an and the: we a thinking of a specific thing
  1. Tim sat down on a chair.
  2. Tim sat down on the chair nearest the door.
  3. Can you turn off the light, please?
  4. I took a taxi to the station.
  5. I have to go to the bank and then I'm going to the post office.
  6. Is there a bank near here?
  1. Tim se sentó en una silla.
  2. Tim se sentó en la silla mas cercana a la puerta.
  3. Puedes apagar la luz, por favor?
  4. Tome un taxi hacia la estación.
  5. Tengo que ir al banco y despues a la oficina de correos.
    (Se puede decir: Tengo que ir un banco (cualquiera).)
  6. ¿Hay un banco cerca de aquí?
The: when there is only one of something
  1. Have you ever crossed the equator?
  2. What is the longest river in Europe?
  3. Our apartment is on the tenth floor.
  4. The internet changed the way we live.
  5. The sun is a star.
  1. Has atravezado el ecuador?
  2. ¿Cuál es el rio más largo de Europa?
  3. Nuestro apartamento está en el quinto piso.
  4. El Internet cambio nuestra vida.
  5. El sol es una estrella.
(go to) the cinema, the theatre; the radio, ∅ television/TV
  1. I go to the cinema al lot, but I haven't been to the theatre for ages.
  2. I listen to the radio a lot.
  3. I watch ∅ television a lot.
  4. We watched it on ∅ TV.
  5. Can you turn of the TV, please? (the television set)
  1. Voy mucho al cine, pero hace mucho no he ido al teatro.
  2. Yo escucho mucho ∅ radio.
    Se puede decir: Yo escucho mucho la radio (una emisora específica).
  3. Miro mucha ∅ televisión.
  4. Lo vimos en ∅ televisión.
  5. ¿Podrías apagar la radio, por favor?
∅ breakfast/lunch/dinner
  • What did you have for ∅ breakfast?
  • We had ∅ lunch in a very nice restaurant.
  • We had a very nice (big/wonderful/early) lunch.
  • ¿Qué te sirvieron de ∅ desayuno?
  • ¿Qué preparaste de ∅ almuerzo?
  • ¿Qué comiste de ∅ cena?
  • Desayuné bien ayer.
  • ¿Preparaste el almuezo?
∅ noun + number: Room 126, page 29, question 3, Gate 10
  • Our train leaves from ∅ Platform 5.
  • Do you have these shoes in ∅ size 43?
  • ¿Tiene estos zapatos en ∅ talla 6?
  • Normalmente uso ∅ talla 6.
  • Soy de ∅ talla 6.
  • La talla 6 no me sirve.
  • No me sirve la talla 6 (de los zapatos que me ofrecieron).
  • Estoy en la página 27.
  • ¿Puedes leer la pregunta 3?
  • Estoy en la habitación 201.
  • El tren salió de la plataforma 14.
general idea of a place: ∅ school, prison, jail, hospital, university, college, church, etc.
  • Sally's father goes to ∅ church every Sunday. (to take part in a religious service)
  • Some workmen went to the church to repair the roof. (not for a religious service)
  • Joe had an accident last week. He was taken to ∅ hospital. He was taken to ∅ hospital. He's still in ∅ hospital now. (as a patient)
  • Jane has gone to the hospital to visit Joe. She's at the hospital now. (as a visitor, not as patient)
  • El papá de Juana va a la iglesia todos los domingos.
  • El papá de Juana va a ∅ misa todos los domingos.
  • Unos obreros fueron a la iglesia para repara el techo.
  • Voy a ∅ clase de español todo los sábados.
to to bed / be in bed etc.
  1. I'm going to ∅ bed now. Goodnight.
  2. Do you ever have breakfast in ∅ bed?
  3. I sat down on the bed. (specific piece of furniture)
go to work / to be at work / start work / finish work etc.
  1. Chris didn't go to ∅ work yesterday.
  2. What time do you usually finish ∅ work?
go home / come home / arrive home / get home / be at home etc
  1. It's late. Let's go ∅ home.
  2. Will you be at ∅ home tomorrow afternoon?
go/be on a voyage: go to sea / be at sea
  1. Keith works on ships. He's at ∅ sea most of the time.
  2. I'd like to live neat the sea.
  3. It can be dangerous to swim in the sea.
when we are talking about things or people in general: ∅
  1. I'm afraid of dogs.
  2. Crime is a problem in most big cities.
  3. Life has changes a lot in the last thirty years.
  4. My favourite subject at school was history.
  5. Do you like classical music?
  6. Most shops accept credit cards?
  7. I like working with people.
  8. I like the people I work with.
  9. I couldn't live without music.
  10. The film wasn't very good, but I liked the music.
  11. The giraffe is the tallest of all animals.
  12. The bicycle is an excellent means of transport.
  13. When was the telephone invented?
The + adjective: the young, the old, the rich, the poor, the sick, the dead, the homeless, the unemployed
  1. Do you think that the rich should pay higher taxes?
  2. We need to do more to help to the homeless.
The + nationality
  1. The French are famous for their food.
  2. The Chineese invented printing.

  4. The United States of America (the USA)
